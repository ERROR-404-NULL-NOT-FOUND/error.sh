The Thinkpad T420 in 2023

A review of Lenovo's Thinkpad T420 from 2011

Overview

The T420 is a buisness laptop that released in 2011.
Being a buisness laptop, it has all of the niceties you would expect; it's well-built with an acceptable screen and outstanding keyboard, as well as being extremely repairible and upgradable.
The price is amazing; depending on where you live, you can get one for as little as $50 with shipping (this is what I paid for mine).
The I/O is amazing, with 4 USB ports (USB 2.0 though...), VGA and DisplayPort out, SD combo and ExpressCard slots, an RJ45 jack, an IEEE 1394 port (more commonly known as FireWire), and a DVD drive.
My unit has a "high-res" 1600x900 screen, 12gb of ram (which I upgraded from the original 4), a 500GB Intel SSD, and an Intel Core i5-2520m processor, as well as a fingerprint reader that I installed.
The performance is ok; I don't have a model with a dedicated GPU, so video playback is a little taxing on the CPU, but other than that it works fine for most web browsing.
Compilation, however, is a bit slow, as should be expected from a CPU of this age.

My experiences

I've had this laptop for a little over a year at this point, and it's held up quite well.
3 days after I got it, it fell from desk height onto a concrete floor, from which it sustained minor cosmethetic damage but nothing to impede functionality.
I've also gone through the effort of installing Coreboot on it, which was a major improvement over the stock BIOS, if only just because it means that I don't have to wait on the Thinkpad start screen.
The first time that I opened it, I was amazed at how solid it was and how good the keyboard felt.
Overall, my experience with the machine has been overwhelmingling positive, with a few downsides:

  • The battery life has been rather poor for me, although that may just be my third-party battery that seems to be surprisingly bad
  • The basketball surface of the touchpad sucks, so I scraped it off with my fingernail
  • On my unit, the DVD drive keeps falling out, but I'm going to blame that on the previous owner
